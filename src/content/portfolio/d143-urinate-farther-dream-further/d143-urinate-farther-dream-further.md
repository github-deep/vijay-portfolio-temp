Bill, please. Francis gestured to the waiter.

'How will you go home?'
'I'll walk', I said as a matter of fact.
'I'll drop you.'
'You are drunk too.'
'Not as much as you are.' Did he mean that his stamina is more than mine?

'It is barely a kilometer, I'll go through the by lanes, no one is around at this time', I insisted.

*

I rode pillion on his bike. I could barely walk straight exiting the bar.

'I need to pee.'

As we both stood urinating, the fresh cold breeze stirred me up.

“I want to go further in life… very far.”

'For now, urinate farther', he said, urinating, adjusting his aim.

He finished before me and fetched a water bottle to wash hands. Meanwhile, I lit a cigarette.

'I have to do something big in life… an impact.'

He kept his hand on my shoulder, and looked straight into my eyes.

'One failure won't stop you.'

We hugged and rode off. A couple of minutes later, he dropped me home and left. I took the elevator, entered the house, and directly crashed on my bed.

I took out my phone and called Francis. 'I just reached safely. Don't worry.'

'Bhosdike, I dropped you'.
