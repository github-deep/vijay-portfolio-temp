This morning, I was cycling on the outskirts of the city. I stopped to drink water when, coincidentally, I ran into an old friend. She recognized me immediately and so did I. We exchanged pleasantries.

'I stay nearby, come over for a coffee.' I had cycled 25 kms by then; a coffee wouldn't hurt, and the old friendship.

She was primarily my ex-girlfriend's friend. I hadn't taken a liking to her then. She was a tad orthodox, and always suspicious of me and probably had filled my ex-girlfriend's ears about me.

She opened the house gate for me to get in. I promptly got by bicycle in first. She looked at my bicycle, and her eyes lit up.

'Oh, the same bicycle.' She took a breath. And as we walked into her home, she added,

'Your cycle took your weight and uncertainty all these years! She couldn't.'

There were no emotions on her face. She said it as a matter of fact. I stood there frozen. The layers in her casual remark were astounding. My mind dissected her words, like a dog digs through the soil. I couldn't move.

'Come in', she called out from the doorstep.

Gathering myself, I walked in and sat on the sofa, as she disappeared into the kitchen. My eyes scanned her house. It was lovely house – orderly, opulent, and comfortable. She has done well with her intelligence, I assumed.

'Where's your husband?', I enquired as she handed me the coffee cup.

'We are going through a divorce. Next month 25th is the final hearing.'

I wanted to feel sorry for her, I wanted to sympathize, I wanted to fill her with strength, I wanted to say that life happens, that this is just a blip. Instead, my tongue rolled –

'So much for your certainty', I said crossing my legs, and gulping a large dose of coffee. Silence replaced the aroma of coffee.
