More often than not, we know the cost-benefit of our deeds, we know the pros-cons of our actions. It is the evolutionary brain at work, keeping survival and needs at the top. And it's raw and beautiful.

But there are situations when I'm in quandary. Especially when people are at the receiving end of my decisions. To do or not? To go or not? To stay or not? To jump or not? To be unbiased or not? These are tough, crossroad decisions to make. Choosing either will put me in diametrically opposing paths.

Dealing with one such difficult problem, I remembered my mentor and also a dearest friend. Whenever I'm stuck in a difficult situation, he is always there to guide me, and guide with logic and reasoning.

The situation at my hand is – Should I cycle from Kolkata to Pune (2000 kms) in April in blistering heat when the temperature touches 45 degree Celsius with ensuing heat wave?

I'm borrowing his advice given in some other context, but I guess it fits in any context. He quite simply would have said – 'You do your job. Nature will do hers.'

That one advice has stayed with me. It essentially means that in any decision I make, I can't control the outcome. But I can definitely control my actions.

***

I recently met Pointer, a friend, a loving mother, and a woman with clear thoughts. (I envy her people-handling skills.) She told me something that echoed my mentor's words. She said, 'You do you'.

So, when in doubt, I DO I!
